# Share Your Story — 5-Minute Demo Script

> **Team members speaking**: Assign roles below. [PRESENTER] is the primary narrator. [DEMO] is the person driving the screen. These can be the same person. [TEAMMATE] is whoever will do the multilingual demo.

---

## PART 1: The Hook (0:00–0:45)

**[PRESENTER]:**

"Think about your grandmother — or your grandfather, an uncle, a neighbor who always had a story. Now think about how many of those stories you actually know. How they met. What scared them as a kid. What they're proudest of.

For most of us, the answer is: not enough. And every year, more of those stories disappear forever. Not because people don't want to share them — but because nobody sat down and asked in the right way.

Questionnaires feel like paperwork. Formal interviews feel awkward. The best stories come out the way they always have — in a real conversation, at the kitchen table, where someone just says, 'Tell me about when you were a kid.'

That's what we built. **Share Your Story** is a conversational storytelling app that sits with you like a friend and helps you capture your life story — in your own voice, in your own words."

---

## PART 2: Live Demo (0:45–3:30)

### 2A: The Library (0:45–1:15)

**[PRESENTER]** *(while [DEMO] shows the Welcome screen):*

"When you open the app, you see your story library. Each person gets their own shelf, and each story is a book on that shelf.

You can start a **guided story** — which walks you through chapters of your life, from childhood to reflections — or a **freeform story**, where you just talk about whatever's on your mind."

**[DEMO]:** Click "New Person", type a name, then click "Begin Guided Story."

---

### 2B: A Conversation (1:15–2:15)

**[PRESENTER]:**

"Let's start a chapter. We'll pick **Childhood** — 'Where your story starts.'

Notice the AI doesn't hand you a form. It opens with something warm and simple — like a friend sitting down with you."

**[DEMO]:** Click the Childhood chapter. Wait for the AI's opening message. Let it play through TTS.

**[PRESENTER]:**

"Now here's the key — you just talk. Press the mic button and speak naturally."

**[DEMO]:** Hit the record button. Speak a short childhood memory (15-20 sec). Something like:

> *"I grew up in a small town in [STATE]. My earliest memory is probably sitting on my grandpa's porch in the summer — he had this old rocking chair and he'd tell me stories about working on the railroad. I must have been about four or five."*

Wait for transcription, then AI response.

**[PRESENTER]** *(while waiting / after response):*

"A few things just happened. The app transcribed the voice in real time, sent it to Claude, and got a response — not a generic 'That's wonderful!' — an actual follow-up that picks up on the details. It caught the mention of grandpa's railroad stories and wants to hear more. It's not running down a checklist — it's listening.

And in the background, Claude is also cleaning up filler words — the 'ums' and 'you knows' — while keeping the speaker's actual voice and personality."

---

### 2C: Multilingual Demo (2:15–2:45)

**[PRESENTER]:**

"And because stories don't only happen in English — the app supports **32 languages**. [TEAMMATE]'s going to show us."

**[TEAMMATE]:** *(Steps in, hits record, and speaks a short memory in [LANGUAGE] — just 2-3 sentences. Something personal and genuine.)*

Wait for transcription to appear.

**[PRESENTER]:**

"Same experience — natural conversation, instant transcription, AI responds in context. A grandmother in Mexico City, a grandfather in Seoul, a dad in Mumbai — they can all tell their story in the language they think and feel in."

---

### 2D: The Reader (2:45–3:10)

**[DEMO]:** Navigate to a **pre-populated story** in the Reader.

**[PRESENTER]:**

"Now, all of this becomes a book. Here's a story we've already been building — you can see chapters in the table of contents, flip through pages, and read the full narrative.

You can toggle between the **original transcript** — exactly what was said — and the **polished version**, which cleans up the grammar but keeps their voice. This isn't AI-generated text. These are real words from a real person, just lightly edited.

This is the thing you print out, hand to your kids, and put on the shelf."

---

### 2E: Export & Sharing (3:10–3:30)

**[DEMO]:** Show the export / share functionality.

**[PRESENTER]:**

"And stories aren't meant to stay locked on one device. You can export to PDF for a physical copy, or share directly to social media. We think storytelling builds community — when you share a memory of your grandmother's cooking, it reminds someone else of theirs. That's the ripple effect we're going for."

---

## PART 3: AI & Ethics (3:30–4:15)

**[PRESENTER]:**

"Let's talk about how we use AI, because in a project about human stories, this matters.

**Claude is the listener, not the author.** It never writes your story for you. It never makes things up. It asks follow-ups, it respects when you want to pause, and it doesn't force fake enthusiasm. It's designed to feel like a friend, not an interviewer.

We were deliberate about this. The prompts tell Claude: 'Follow their tangents — that's where the real story lives. Don't interrupt. Don't push.' If someone brings up something painful — a loss, a regret — the AI is gentle. It doesn't probe.

**Your data stays yours.** Stories are stored locally on your device. Nothing is sent to a server you don't control. You can delete any story, any conversation, at any time.

The creative track asks: is AI empowering people or replacing them? Our answer is clear — the human is always the storyteller. AI is just making it easier to have the conversation."

---

## PART 4: What's Next (4:15–4:45)

**[PRESENTER]:**

"We built this in a weekend, but the vision is bigger.

**Family sharing** — imagine inviting your siblings to add their own chapters to the same book. Mom's version of the camping trip next to Dad's. Your cousins remembering the same holiday differently. One family, many voices.

**Language preservation** — with 32 languages already supported, this could help communities capture stories in endangered languages before they're lost.

And **content sensitivity** — options for handling difficult topics like trauma or loss with extra care, so people feel safe sharing the hard parts too."

---

## PART 5: Close (4:45–5:00)

**[PRESENTER]:**

"We all have someone whose story we wish we'd captured. A voice we wish we could still hear. We can't go back — but we can make sure the stories that are still here don't get lost.

**Share Your Story** — because everyone has a story worth preserving."
